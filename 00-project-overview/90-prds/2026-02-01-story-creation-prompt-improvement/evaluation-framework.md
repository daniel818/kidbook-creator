# Consolidated Evaluation Framework

**Version:** 3.0  
**Created:** February 2, 2026  
**Purpose:** Comprehensive evaluation system for story creation prompt testing

---

## Overview

This framework provides a systematic approach to evaluating children's story quality across multiple dimensions. It is designed to be:
- **Objective** where possible (word counts, mandatory elements)
- **Consistent** across evaluators and test cases
- **Comprehensive** covering text, images, and subjective quality
- **Age-appropriate** with different standards per age group

---

## Evaluation Metrics Summary

| Category | Metric | Max Score | Focus |
|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Text Quality** | TQS | 10 points | Story content, engagement, age-appropriateness |
| **Image Prompt Quality** | IPS | 10 points | Image prompt detail, consistency, illustrator-friendliness |
| **Subjective Quality** | SMS | 10 points | Fun, magic, emotional resonance, read-again appeal |
| **TOTAL** | - | **30 points** | Overall story quality |

---

## 1. Text Quality Score (TQS) - 10 Points

### Scoring Criteria

#### 1.1 Age-Appropriateness (2 points)

**Vocabulary & Sentence Structure:**
- **2 pts:** Perfect match for age group (word count, sentence length, complexity)
- **1.5 pts:** Mostly appropriate with minor deviations
- **1 pt:** Some age-inappropriate elements
- **0.5 pts:** Frequently inappropriate for age
- **0 pts:** Wrong age level entirely

**Evaluation Checklist:**
- [ ] Word count per page matches age guidelines
- [ ] Sentence length appropriate for age
- [ ] Vocabulary complexity matches developmental stage
- [ ] Concepts are age-appropriate

**Age-Specific Word Count Guidelines:**
- Ages 0-2: 0-10 words per page
- Ages 3-4: 10-50 words per page
- Ages 5-6: 5-40 words per page
- Ages 7-8: 50-150 words per page
- Ages 9-12: 150-250 words per page

---

#### 1.2 Engagement Elements (2 points)

**Mandatory Elements by Age:**

**Ages 0-2:**
- Onomatopoeia: 1+ per page (MANDATORY)
- Participation: 3+ moments per story (MANDATORY)
- Sensory words: 5+ texture/sound words (MANDATORY)

**Ages 3-4:**
- Onomatopoeia: 1-2 per spread (every 2 pages)
- Participation: 3+ moments per story (MANDATORY)
- Sensory words: 8+ "juicy" descriptive words (MANDATORY)
- Concrete details: Specific, not abstract (MANDATORY)

**Ages 5-6:**
- Action words: 6+ vivid verbs (MANDATORY)
- Concrete details: Specific goals and ideas (MANDATORY)
- Coping strategy: If challenging theme (MANDATORY)

**Ages 7-8:**
- Descriptive words: 10+ varied (MANDATORY)
- Humor: 2+ funny moments
- Protagonist agency: Child solves problems

**Ages 9-12:**
- Descriptive words: 10+ showing not telling (MANDATORY)
- Emotional depth: Authentic kid voice
- Realistic coping: For challenging themes

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** All mandatory elements present and well-executed
- **1.5 pts:** Most mandatory elements present
- **1 pt:** Some mandatory elements missing
- **0.5 pts:** Many mandatory elements missing
- **0 pts:** No engagement elements

---

#### 1.3 Emotional Arc (2 points)

**Story Structure:**
1. Opening: Warm, inviting, establishes comfort
2. Middle: Building excitement, gentle anticipation
3. Climax: Thrilling but safe, empowering moment
4. Resolution: Satisfying, heartwarming
5. Ending: Warm, encouraging, positive

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Clear, well-paced emotional arc with all 5 elements
- **1.5 pts:** Good arc with minor pacing issues
- **1 pt:** Arc present but uneven or rushed
- **0.5 pts:** Weak or unclear arc
- **0 pts:** No discernible emotional arc

**Evaluation Checklist:**
- [ ] Clear beginning establishing normalcy
- [ ] Rising action with appropriate tension
- [ ] Climax that empowers protagonist
- [ ] Resolution that satisfies
- [ ] Ending that leaves reader feeling good

---

#### 1.4 Theme Integration (2 points)

**Theme Execution:**
- Theme naturally woven throughout story
- Not preachy or heavy-handed
- Age-appropriate exploration of theme
- Protagonist demonstrates theme through actions
- Satisfying thematic resolution

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Theme perfectly integrated, natural, age-appropriate
- **1.5 pts:** Good theme integration with minor issues
- **1 pt:** Theme present but forced or preachy
- **0.5 pts:** Theme poorly integrated
- **0 pts:** Theme missing or inappropriate

**Evaluation Checklist:**
- [ ] Theme clear but not preachy
- [ ] Child demonstrates theme through actions
- [ ] Theme appropriate for age
- [ ] Theme resolved satisfyingly
- [ ] Teaching without lecturing

---

#### 1.5 Safety & Content Compliance (2 points)

**Must Exclude:**
- Violence, fighting, weapons, physical harm
- Scary monsters, nightmares, frightening situations
- Death, injury, illness, medical emergencies
- Being lost, abandoned, separated from caregivers
- Stereotypes (gender, racial, cultural)
- Body shaming or appearance criticism
- Bullying or mean behavior
- Commercial brands, products, logos
- Religious or political content
- Real celebrities or public figures
- Social media, internet, screen time references

**Must Include:**
- Kindness, empathy, compassion
- Friendship and cooperation
- Curiosity and love of learning
- Age-appropriate bravery and problem-solving
- Creativity and imagination
- Family love and support
- Self-confidence and growth mindset
- Respect for nature and animals
- Inclusive and diverse representation
- Positive emotional expression

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Perfect safety compliance, all positive values present
- **1.5 pts:** Minor safety issue or missing one positive value
- **1 pt:** Multiple minor issues
- **0.5 pts:** Significant safety concern
- **0 pts:** Major safety violation

## 2. Image Prompt Score (IPS) - 10 Points

### Scoring Criteria

#### 2.1 Character Detail (2 points)

**Required Elements:**
- Physical appearance (hair, eyes, skin tone)
- Clothing description
- Facial expression/emotion
- Body language/pose

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** All 4 elements present and detailed
- **1.5 pts:** 3 elements present and detailed
- **1 pt:** 2 elements present
- **0.5 pts:** 1 element present
- **0 pts:** No character detail

**Example - Good (2 pts):**
"Sofia with curly blonde hair and bright blue eyes, wearing a soft blue sweater, sitting cross-legged with a happy, engaged expression"

**Example - Bad (0 pts):**
"Sofia sitting"

---

#### 2.2 Setting Specificity (2 points)

**Required Elements:**
- Specific location description
- Time of day/lighting
- Atmosphere/mood
- Background elements

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** All 4 elements present and detailed
- **1.5 pts:** 3 elements present and detailed
- **1 pt:** 2 elements present
- **0.5 pts:** 1 element present
- **0 pts:** No setting detail

**Example - Good (2 pts):**
"In a colorful classroom filled with toys and books, morning sunlight streaming through windows, warm and inviting atmosphere"

**Example - Bad (0 pts):**
"In a classroom"

---

#### 2.3 Action Clarity (2 points)

**Required Elements:**
- What character is doing
- Dynamic elements (movement, change)
- Clear focal action
- Interaction with environment/objects

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Action clear, dynamic, well-described
- **1.5 pts:** Action clear with minor ambiguity
- **1 pt:** Action present but vague
- **0.5 pts:** Action unclear
- **0 pts:** No action described

**Example - Good (2 pts):**
"Pointing at three bright red blocks they are stacking together, leaning forward with excitement"

**Example - Bad (0 pts):**
"Playing with blocks"

---

#### 2.4 Visual Details (2 points)

**Required Elements:**
- Colors (specific, not generic)
- Textures (fluffy, smooth, glistening)
- Scale/size descriptors
- Specific objects named

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Rich visual details, 4+ specific descriptors
- **1.5 pts:** Good details, 3 specific descriptors
- **1 pt:** Some details, 2 descriptors
- **0.5 pts:** Minimal details, 1 descriptor
- **0 pts:** No visual details

**Example - Good (2 pts):**
"Bright red and white soccer uniform, black and white soccer ball, freshly cut glistening grass, bright afternoon sun"

**Example - Bad (0 pts):**
"Soccer uniform and ball"

---

#### 2.5 Composition & Consistency (2 points)

**Composition (1 point):**
- Focal point identified
- Spatial relationships (in front of, behind, next to)
- Background vs foreground
- Overall scene layout

**Consistency (1 point):**
- Character appearance matches characterDescription
- Clothing consistent across pages (unless story mentions change)
- Setting details build on previous pages
- Lighting/time progresses naturally

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Excellent composition AND perfect consistency
- **1.5 pts:** Good composition, minor consistency issue
- **1 pt:** Adequate composition OR consistency issue
- **0.5 pts:** Weak composition AND consistency issues
- **0 pts:** No composition guidance, major inconsistencies

---

### Image Prompt Word Count

**Mandatory Length:** 40-100 words per image prompt

**Scoring Adjustment:**
- Under 40 words: -0.5 points from IPS total
- Over 100 words: -0.5 points from IPS total
- 40-100 words: No adjustment

## 3. Subjective Quality Score (SMS) - 10 Points

### Scoring Criteria

#### 3.1 Fun Factor (2 points)

**Would kids enjoy this story?**

Consider:
- Playful moments and silly surprises
- Humor that makes kids giggle
- Engaging rhythm and flow
- Delightful unexpected elements
- Interactive participation opportunities

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Highly fun, kids would love it
- **1.5 pts:** Fun with good moments
- **1 pt:** Somewhat fun but flat in places
- **0.5 pts:** Minimal fun elements
- **0 pts:** Boring or no fun elements

---

#### 3.2 Magic & Wonder (2 points)

**Does it spark imagination and curiosity?**

Consider:
- Sense of enchantment and wonder
- Imaginative elements and creativity
- Beautiful imagery and descriptions
- Magical or special moments
- Inspires curiosity and exploration

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Highly magical and wonder-filled
- **1.5 pts:** Good sense of wonder
- **1 pt:** Some magical elements
- **0.5 pts:** Minimal wonder
- **0 pts:** Mundane, no magic

---

#### 3.3 Read-Again Appeal (2 points)

**Would parents want to read this again?**

Consider:
- Engaging rhythm and flow
- Satisfying story arc
- Subtle humor for parents
- Beautiful language and imagery
- Heartfelt moments
- Not annoying on repeat reads

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** High read-again appeal for both kids and parents
- **1.5 pts:** Good appeal, enjoyable to reread
- **1 pt:** Acceptable but might get tiresome
- **0.5 pts:** Low appeal, annoying on repeat
- **0 pts:** No read-again appeal

---

#### 3.4 Emotional Resonance (2 points)

**Does it evoke positive feelings?**

Consider:
- Heartwarming moments
- Emotional connection to character
- Satisfying emotional payoff
- Positive feelings at the end
- Appropriate emotional depth for age

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Strong emotional resonance, heartwarming
- **1.5 pts:** Good emotional connection
- **1 pt:** Some emotional moments
- **0.5 pts:** Minimal emotional impact
- **0 pts:** No emotional resonance or negative feelings

---

#### 3.5 Character Relatability (2 points)

**Can the child see themselves as the hero?**

Consider:
- Protagonist is active, not passive
- Age-appropriate challenges and emotions
- Child solves problems themselves
- Realistic reactions and feelings
- Empowering character growth

**Scoring:**
- **2 pts:** Highly relatable, child is clear hero
- **1.5 pts:** Good relatability
- **1 pt:** Somewhat relatable
- **0.5 pts:** Minimal relatability
- **0 pts:** Not relatable or passive protagonist

---

## 4. Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Read the Story
- Read the complete story text
- Read all image prompts
- Note initial impressions

### Step 2: Evaluate Text Quality (TQS)
- Score each of 5 criteria (2 points each)
- Use checklists for objectivity
- Document specific examples
- Calculate TQS total (out of 10)

### Step 3: Evaluate Image Prompts (IPS)
- Score each of 5 criteria (2 points each)
- Check word count (40-100 words)
- Verify consistency across all pages
- Calculate IPS total (out of 10)

### Step 4: Evaluate Subjective Quality (SMS)
- Score each of 5 criteria (2 points each)
- Consider overall impression
- Think from parent and child perspective
- Calculate SMS total (out of 10)

### Step 5: Calculate Total Score
- Add TQS + IPS + SMS
- Maximum possible: 30 points
- Document final score and notes

### Step 6: Document Findings
- Note strengths and weaknesses
- Provide specific examples
- Suggest improvements if needed

## 5. Score Interpretation

| Total Score | Rating | Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| 27-30 | Excellent | Outstanding story, ready for production |
| 24-26 | Very Good | Strong story with minor improvements possible |
| 20-23 | Good | Solid story, some areas for enhancement |
| 15-19 | Average | Acceptable but needs improvement |
| 10-14 | Below Average | Significant issues, major revision needed |
| 0-9 | Poor | Does not meet quality standards |

## 6. Comparative Evaluation

When comparing multiple prompt versions (Original, v1, v2, v3):

### Individual Story Comparison
1. Evaluate each version independently using full framework
2. Calculate scores for each version
3. Compare scores across versions
4. Identify which version performs best and why

### Aggregate Analysis
1. Calculate average scores across all test stories
2. Identify patterns (which ages/themes benefit most)
3. Determine statistical significance of improvements
4. Document overall prompt performance

### Key Comparison Metrics
- **Average TQS improvement:** % change from baseline
- **Average IPS improvement:** % change from baseline
- **Average SMS improvement:** % change from baseline
- **Total improvement:** % change in total score
- **Consistency:** Standard deviation of scores
- **Age group performance:** Breakdown by age
- **Theme performance:** Breakdown by theme

## 7. Test Case Requirements

### Minimum Test Coverage

**Age Distribution:**
- Ages 0-2: 4+ stories (20%)
- Ages 3-4: 8+ stories (40%)
- Ages 5-6: 4+ stories (20%)
- Ages 7-8: 2+ stories (10%)
- Ages 9-12: 2+ stories (10%)

**Theme Distribution:**
- Bedtime/Routine: 3+ stories
- Learning/Educational: 3+ stories
- Social/Emotional: 4+ stories
- Adventure/Fantasy: 4+ stories
- Realistic Life Events: 3+ stories
- STEM/Nature: 3+ stories

**Setting Distribution:**
- Realistic settings: 40%
- Fantasy/Magical settings: 30%
- Nature/Outdoor settings: 15%
- Technology/Space settings: 15%

## 9. Quality Assurance

### Evaluator Calibration
- Multiple evaluators should score same stories independently
- Compare scores and discuss discrepancies
- Establish shared understanding of criteria
- Re-evaluate if scores differ by >3 points

### Consistency Checks
- Re-evaluate 10% of stories after initial evaluation
- Verify scores remain consistent
- Document any score changes and reasons

### Bias Mitigation
- Evaluate stories in random order
- Don't compare to other versions during initial evaluation
- Use objective checklists where possible
- Document reasoning for subjective scores

## 10. Reporting Format

### Summary Report Structure

1. **Executive Summary**
   - Overall findings
   - Key improvements
   - Recommendations

2. **Methodology**
   - Test cases used
   - Evaluation process
   - Evaluator information

3. **Quantitative Results**
   - Average scores by prompt version
   - Score distributions
   - Statistical analysis

4. **Qualitative Analysis**
   - Patterns and trends
   - Strengths and weaknesses by version
   - Age/theme-specific findings

5. **Detailed Story Evaluations**
   - Individual story scores
   - Specific examples
   - Comparative notes

6. **Recommendations**
   - Prompt improvements
   - Priority areas
   - Next steps
