## Naming Rationale

### Why "TaleCrafters" Ranks #1

**Strengths**:
- ✅ Emphasizes craft and quality (trustworthy)
- ✅ Active, empowering verb (crafters)
- ✅ Sophisticated but approachable
- ✅ Works well in German: "GeschichtenHandwerker" or keep English
- ✅ Unique, not generic
- ✅ Scalable for future products (TaleCrafters Pro, TaleCrafters Kids)
- ✅ Available domains likely (.com, .de, .co.il)
- ✅ Professional enough for parents, magical enough for concept

**Considerations**:
- Slightly longer than some alternatives
- "Crafters" implies skill/effort (could be positive or negative)

### Why "StoryHeroes" Ranks #2

**Strengths**:
- ✅ Direct child-as-hero concept
- ✅ Empowering and uplifting
- ✅ Easy to remember and spell
- ✅ Translates well internationally
- ✅ Clear value proposition
- ✅ Appeals to both parents and children

**Considerations**:
- More literal, less sophisticated
- "Heroes" is common in kids' content

### Why "MagicTales" Ranks #3

**Strengths**:
- ✅ Simple, memorable, catchy
- ✅ Magical + storytelling combined
- ✅ Easy to spell and pronounce
- ✅ Works across languages
- ✅ Warm and inviting

**Considerations**:
- Very common word combination
- May be harder to secure domain
- Less unique/distinctive

## Trademark Considerations

Before finalizing, conduct trademark search for:
1. Exact name match
2. Similar names in children's books/education category
3. International trademark databases (Germany, Israel, US)

**Higher Risk Names** (common terms):
- StoryMakers
- MagicTales
- HeroBooks

**Lower Risk Names** (more unique):
- TaleCrafters
- TaleSpark
- StoryGlow
